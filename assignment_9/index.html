How to Carve a Pumpkin

Materials Needed:

  • Tablecloth
  • Large Bowl
  • Writing/drawing utensil (ex: Sharpie)
  • Cleaning spray
  • LED lights

Helpful Tools:

  • Ice cream scoop
  • Filet knife
  • Paring knife
  • Lemon zester
  • Vegetable peeler

Step 1:

Prep the carving area

  • Preferably at an outdoor table, cover the surface with a washable tablecloth, craft paper, newspaper, or another tool for quick clean up.

Step 2:

Cut a hole in the back of your pumpkin

  • Cut a square or diamond shape that will be easy to replace back into the pumpkin. Make sure it is large enough to fit your hand into the pumpkin.

    • NOTE: Cutting a hole around the stem, at the top of your pumpkin, will weaken it structurally.

Step 3:

Scoop out the guts

  • Using your hands or the ice cream scoop, remove all the slimy seeds and gooey insides of the pumpkin.
  • Collect in the large bowl and save the seeds for later.

Step 4:

Select your design

  • Sketch your design on your pumpkin using a sharpie or another writing utenstil.
  • Select a stencil to allow ease of carving. Print out a stencil and tape it to your pumpkin to create a guide to carve through.

Step 5:

Start Carving

  • Be sure to use a sharp knife.
  • Carefully cut along your design.
  • Remove pieces of the pumpkin as you go!

Step 6:

Preserve your pumpkin

  • Spray the inside of your pumpkin with a cleaning spray containing bleach.
  • Allow it to evaporate for a minimum of five minutes.

    • NOTE: This keeps the pumpkin from rotting too quickly and deters squirrels and animals from snacking on your pumpkin.

Step 7:

Light it up!

  • Through the access point at the back, add a battery powered flameless tea-light or ball up LED string lights to create a long lasting glow.
